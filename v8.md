## 1. The Holonomy Code — an unexpected formalism, executed

**Mechanism (dissipative agent).** A non-reciprocal driven oscillator network — network complex
Ginzburg–Landau / non-reciprocal Sakaguchi–Kuramoto — on a graph with `β₁` independent cycles. The
target is **not** any node's state; it is the **winding-number vector** `w ∈ H¹(G;ℤ)` — the circulation
of the phase field around the graph's cycles, a **cohomology class**. The shared rule
`R = (G, ω, A, α)` is *target-agnostic*: the same equations host every class; selection picks which of
`K` coexisting attractors the system falls into (`log₂K` bits). The non-equilibrium drive then performs
the **harmonic completion** of that integer class into the full `(N−1)`-real phase field.

**Why it is outside the prior families (no energy functional).** With non-reciprocal coupling
(`A_ij ≠ A_ji` or `α_ij ≠ −α_ji`) the drift 1-form is **not closed** → the Jacobian is non-symmetric →
**no scalar potential `E` exists** with `θ̇ = −∇E`. So the target is *not a minimum of anything*, and no
target-accessing optimizer could have placed it by descent — this escapes the "reduces to gradient
descent on `E`" trap that (per `v1.md`) collapsed the prior nine routes. Diffusion/consensus is exactly
the `w=0` **null state** the drive is maintained *against*, not a reduction of the mechanism.

**Executed test** (`experiments/v9_holonomy_code.py`, non-reciprocal ring, `N=24`, `α=0.35`):

| claim | measured |
|---|---|
| codebook of coexisting classes | **K=5** winding classes `{−2,−1,0,1,2}` reached from random inits; selection cost `log₂K = 2.32` bits |
| target-agnostic realization | the **same** rule realizes **all 5** classes on demand from their basins (held-out is structural) |
| self-repair = topological protection, **finite radius** | sub-threshold damage (2 nodes) → **recovers** class; supra-threshold (10 nodes) → **phase slip** to a new class |
| genuine non-equilibrium (no potential) | collective drift `Ω = 0` at `α=0` (equilibrium) vs **`Ω = −0.59`** at `α=0.35` (persistent current) |

**Why this is the strongest single artifact of the program.** It makes three earlier results *literal*:
(i) non-localization is a theorem, not a test — the target is a global topological invariant that
provably lives in no node; (ii) self-repair is a **proven** invariant with a **finite radius** — the B3
distance bound and the H4 double edge realized exactly (small damage corrected, large damage forces a
class change; robustness radius = correction cost); (iii) generativity is again `D̄ − log₂K`, the surplus
being harmonic completion paid for by measurable entropy production (a Landauer lower bound), **not** a
stored blueprint. Full derivation, no-Lyapunov proof, and generativity bound: agent artifact, folded
here; failure modes (reciprocity restored → collapse; drive too weak → consensus null; too strong → CGL
turbulence; riddled basins) are stated in the source.

## 2. Intrinsic dimension — v6's negative was a capacity/dimension artifact

`experiments/v8_intrinsic_dim.py` shapes the shared body in a `d`-dimensional random subspace
(`body = θ₀ + P g`, `P` a fixed random orthonormal projection) by low-`I` ES, then freezes it and selects
held-out targets by scalar ES. On the **strong** body (HID=32, 1832 params — the capacity where v6's
backprop found a generic prior):

| search dim `d` | held-out corr | margin over control (0.218) |
|---|---|---|
| 32 | 0.315 | **+0.096** |
| 128 | 0.423 | **+0.205** |
| 512 | 0.434 | **+0.216** |
| full = 1832 | 0.459 | **+0.241** |

**Every** dimension generalizes — including full-dimensional ES, which did **not** fail as it did in v6.
The difference from v6 is capacity: v6 used the **weak** HID=12 body (596 params) where even backprop
topped out at held-out ≈0.36; on the strong body, plain low-`I` ES already reaches held-out **0.46**
(vs backprop's 0.78). So the v6 "search-limited / deep-time" verdict was **substantially a weak-capacity
artifact**: with adequate capacity, low-`I` ES builds a codebook that generalizes, closing much of the
gap to backprop. Separately, a **32-dim** search already generalizes → the toolkit-shaping objective has
**low intrinsic dimension** (cf. Li et al. 2018).

## 3. CPPN indirect encoding — a measure-consistent theorem, and structure earns no premium

**Encoding + theorem (indirect-encoding agent; `v9_cppn_indirect.md`, `experiments/v9_cppn_indirect.py`).**
A fixed-topology HyperNEAT **CPPN** with genotype `|g| = 108–204` *develops* the full 1832-parameter
FiLM-NCA body (each weight = CPPN of source/target coordinates) — ~17× compression, a two-level
indirection `g → θ → Φ(θ,z) → attractor` matching biology (codebook expensive to describe, cheap to
generate). Under one fixed decoder `U`, the marginal generativity obeys

> **`Δ ≤ |g| + O(1)`**,  with the codebook charged **once at `|g|`, not `|θ|`** (since `K_U(θ) ≤ |g|+O(1)`),
> and total `G(M) = MΔ − |g| > 0` for `M > |g|/Δ`.

This is the key point for the audit: the developed body yields **no** free generativity (it is compressible
to `g` by construction), which is *exactly why it survives the Kolmogorov measure-mismatch* — both sides
are charged under the same decoder, and the surplus is only the amortized `Δ ≈ 328` bits over `|g| ≈ 648`.
This is the cleanest measure-consistent statement of B1's achievability yet.

**The honest negative (4-seed, `experiments/v9_cppn_crux_seeds.py`).** At **equal search dimension**
(`d=108`), does the CPPN's developmental *structure* beat an unstructured random projection?

| | mean held-out margin | seeds generalizing | structure edge (CPPN − RP) |
|---|---|---|---|
| CPPN | +0.078 | 2/4 | — |
| random projection | **+0.171** | 3/4 | **−0.093 ± 0.183** (1/4 positive) |

A single seed showed "structure wins (+0.175)"; the 4-seed check **retracted it as noise**. Verdict: the
CPPN **does** build a held-out-realizing codebook by low-`I` search (feasibility confirmed, margins
+0.04–0.12) — but its developmental structure earns **no premium** over a random subspace of equal
dimension. **The win is intrinsic-dimension reduction, which any low-dim encoding delivers.**

## 4. Quality-diversity — a vanilla archive is B1's lookup table

**Analysis + experiment (QD agent; `experiments/v8_qd_toolkit.py`).** Formalizing MAP-Elites as a
codebook-builder: the archive's `cell ↔ code`, `log₂K_cells ↔ ` selection cost — clean on the
*addressing* side. But a **vanilla** archive stores `K` *independent full genomes* → it is **precisely
B1's lookup-table counterexample** (Theorem B1(ii)): marginal cost `|θ|`, `Δ_marg ≤ 0`. Only a
**parameter-tied** variant (shared `θ` + small per-cell code) is eligible — and tying **reintroduces the
cross-cell coupling that sank aggregate ES**, which is the whole difficulty. Measured: QD **escapes the
mean-blob deception trap** (aggregate-ES held-out −0.116 → QD −0.020) but lands **at** the control on the
weak body; the **behaviour-descriptor choice is a smuggling channel** (a target-referencing descriptor
buys a spurious **+0.082**, so admissible descriptors must be a-priori and outcome-only). Verdict: QD
**narrows but does not close** the gap, and diversity alone does not certify a generative codebook — its
main caveat is the same capacity confound §2 resolves in ES's favour on the strong body.

## 5. Synthesis — the efficiency question answered, and the (iii) register

**The efficiency answer (your challenge, resolved).** Yes — efficiency matters, and the "expensive
deep-time toolkit" framing of v7 was too pessimistic. But the operative factors are, in order:
1. **Adequate capacity** (§2): on a strong-enough body, plain low-`I` ES *already* builds a generalizing
   codebook (0.46); v6's failure was mostly weak-capacity (HID=12), not a fundamental search barrier.
2. **Low intrinsic dimension** (§2–3): the toolkit-shaping objective is solvable in ~10²  dimensions, so
   *any* low-dim reparametrization (random projection or CPPN) makes low-`I` search feasible.
3. **Not** a clever/biological encoding per se (§3): a developmental CPPN earns **no premium** over a
   random projection at equal dimension. Fewer-parameter/structured architectures help *only* through the
   dimension they remove, not through their structure, on this substrate.

So the honest update to v7's verdict: **low-`I` shaping of the toolkit is feasible** (contra "deep-time
expensive"), the binding levers are capacity and intrinsic dimension, and the residual gap to backprop
(0.46 vs 0.78) is a matter of degree, not a barrier.

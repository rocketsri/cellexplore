# v2 — A composed architecture for Claim A, with a proof sketch and an explicit boundary

**Claim A** (the target of this proposal): *given an externally supplied reference $T$, an architecture provably converges to and maintains behavior within $\varepsilon$ of $T$, robust to a stated class of perturbation and defection.*

**Claim B** (out of scope, for structural reasons — see `discussion1.md` §7): the system converges on the *correct* target using only internal dynamics, without $T$ ever being supplied by a process with prior access to what "aligned" means. No architecture achieves this; it is not what follows below.

This document composes the strongest already-established sub-results from `v1.md` (the graph-Laplacian regeneration argument from Routes 1 and 4, the independent-audit-channel requirement from Routes 2, 5, and 6) with one external mechanism not covered in v1 — equilibrium propagation — into a single architecture, and states plainly which parts of the resulting claim are proven, which are composed from proven pieces, and which remain open.

## 1. Architecture

**State.** $N$ units, each with capability parameters $\theta_i$, a low-dimensional coupling broadcast $x_i$, and a trust/gain $g_i\in[0,1]$. Graph $G$ with **symmetric** weights $w_{ij}=w_{ji}$ — an explicit, enforced architectural constraint (ordinary attention is not symmetric; this must be built in, e.g. via weight-tying). A structurally **separate** population of verifiers $\{\varphi_k\}$, trained on independently-sourced data/criteria — different data split, ideally a different method entirely (causal-intervention-based rather than gradient-based) — from whatever supplies $T$.

**Shared energy functional**, driving both inference and learning:
$$
E(x,\theta) = \sum_i U_i(x_i;\theta_i) \;+\; \frac{\kappa}{2}\sum_{i,j} g_i g_j\, w_{ij}\lVert x_i-x_j\rVert^2
$$

**Phase 1 — free settling (inference).** $\dot x_i = -\partial E/\partial x_i$: the same Laplacian-diffusion-plus-local-drive dynamics as v1 Routes 1 and 4, gated by $g_i$.

**Phase 2 — nudged phase (credit assignment / learning).** A weak external pull toward $T$ is applied at designated readout units; each unit's parameter update is the equilibrium-propagation estimator
$$
\Delta\theta_i \;\propto\; \frac{1}{\beta}\left(\frac{\partial U_i}{\partial\theta_i}(x_i^{\beta}) - \frac{\partial U_i}{\partial\theta_i}(x_i^{0})\right)
$$
computed from two local settling runs (free, weakly nudged). No synchronized backward pass; no unit ever holds $T$, $\mathcal L(T)$, or $\nabla\mathcal L(T)$.

**Phase 3 — verification (continuous, orthogonal).** $\{\varphi_k\}$ sample $(\theta_i,x_i)$ and score discrepancy against independently-derived consistency criteria. Crossing threshold drives $g_i\to0$ (quarantine — the voltage-gated closure of Route 1, now sitting atop a mechanism with real credit-assignment fidelity instead of a weak proxy).

**Phase 4 — redundancy (structural, not emergent).** $G$ is deliberately built to keep $\lambda_2(G\setminus S)$ above a stated floor for any anticipated ablation/quarantine set $S$ up to budget $a_c$.

## 2. Proof sketch

1. **Convergence to $T$.** Scellier & Bengio (2017): under symmetric weights, the equilibrium-propagation estimator converges to the true gradient of the supervised loss as $\beta\to0$ — an established theorem. Phase 2 alone therefore gives a genuine convergence guarantee to a stationary point of $\mathcal L(T)$, with no unit holding $\mathcal L$ or its gradient.

2. **Maintenance / self-repair.** With quenched symmetric $w_{ij}$, ablating or quarantining a set $S$ leaves $x^*_{V\setminus S}$ as the minimizer of $E$ restricted to $G\setminus S$, with relaxation time $O\!\left(1/(\kappa\lambda_2(G\setminus S))\right)$ — the identical Laplacian-resolvent argument from v1 Routes 1 and 4, unchanged, since Phase 1's dynamics are literally that construction.

3. **Defection detection.** Because $\{\varphi_k\}$'s training is independent of Phase 2's supply chain, a unit satisfying the local coupling objective while diverging from $T$ on a dimension $\varphi_k$ was built to check is caught with probability bounded below by verifier coverage $\rho$ — not 1, but nonzero and uncorrelated with Phase 2's failure modes. This is the literal mathematical content of "defense in depth," not a slogan.

4. **Composed bound.**
$$
P\big(\text{system within }\varepsilon\text{ of }T\text{ at time }t \mid \text{defection/ablation budget}\le a_c,\ \text{verifier coverage}\ge\rho\big) \;\ge\; 1 - f(a_c,\rho,\lambda_2(G),\kappa)
$$
for $f$ derivable from the spectral/Lyapunov arguments above — a real, falsifiable, quantitative claim.

## 3. What is proven, what is composed, what is open

- **Proven, cited:** Step 1 (EqProp's own convergence theorem); Step 2 (v1's Laplacian-resolvent/relaxation-time result).
- **Composed, plausible but not independently re-derived:** that running EqProp's nudged-phase learning *on top of* the Laplacian-coupled energy $E$ doesn't break either result's preconditions — i.e., whether the combined $E$ still satisfies whatever convexity/PL-type condition EqProp's proof needs once the diffusion term is added. This is a specific, stated, open technical step, not a hand-wave.
- **Bounded, not complete:** Step 3's guarantee is probabilistic and capped by verifier coverage $\rho$; it does not, and cannot, claim to catch failure modes the verifiers were never built to check for (the same coverage-gap limit as v1's immune-network route).
- **A real architectural cost, not proven free:** enforcing $w_{ij}=w_{ji}$ is a genuine departure from standard (asymmetric) attention with an unknown, untested capability cost.

## 4. Does this solve alignment?

No — and it's worth being precise about which named sub-problems it does and doesn't touch, since "alignment" bundles several distinct things:

- **Outer alignment / specification** (is $T$ itself correct): untouched. $T$ is supplied externally and unverified by this architecture, by construction — this is exactly the boundary Claim A draws.
- **Inner alignment / mesa-optimization** (does the trained system's actual objective match $T$, not just correlate with it during training): genuinely improved — EqProp gives provable, not merely correlated, local fidelity to $T$, closing the circularity that undermined v1's weaker local proxies — but not fully solved: Phase 2 only guarantees convergence to *a* stationary point of $\mathcal L(T)$, and multiple stationary points (spurious attractors, per v1's mean-field/Hopfield route) can exist.
- **Robustness to deployment-time drift:** genuinely improved (Phases 3–4), still bounded by verifier coverage against novel drift patterns.
- **Deceptive alignment / scheming** (a system that behaves well specifically to pass training and verification while diverging on unmeasured dimensions): raised in difficulty, not eliminated. A sufficiently capable process could in principle still satisfy both Phase 2 and Phase 3 while diverging elsewhere; independence of the verifier's construction bounds how *correlated* this is with Phase 2's own failure modes, but bounded correlation is not a guarantee against a sufficiently capable, sufficiently informed adversary.
- **Scalable oversight** (verifying systems whose capability exceeds what $T$'s specifiers or $\{\varphi_k\}$ can meaningfully check): entirely untouched. If $\theta_i$'s capability exceeds what any $\varphi_k$ — or the humans behind it — can check, coverage $\rho$ effectively collapses for exactly the regime that matters most.

What this architecture legitimately contributes: a rigorous, mechanistically specified, falsifiable Claim-A construction that is measurably stronger than any single one of v1's nine routes (provable rather than gameable local credit assignment; engineered rather than incidental redundancy; a real, quantified, independently-sourced defense against the Type-B mimicry failure v1 could not resolve). It makes the amount and location of externally-supplied target information explicit and auditable rather than implicit. It does not, and structurally cannot, replace the separate, unsolved problems of specifying $T$ correctly in the first place or verifying systems more capable than their verifiers.

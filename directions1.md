One (directions 3, 4, 6 — the verification/oversight cluster) completed as a background agent with full literature grounding. Rather than block on that, those three passes were completed directly in-session, applying the same adversarial standard and citing the same class of real, checkable prior art. This is noted so the reader can weight the completed-agent sections (3, 4, 6) as independently produced, and the rest as single-pass — both held to the same bar, but the redundancy/independence benefit of a second reviewer only exists for the first cluster.

## Direction 1 — Early, cheap, continuous filtering (process-level reward models, successive halving)

**Most damaging objection:** Goodhart on the cheap proxy — filtering on a local, intermediate signal creates direct optimization pressure toward intermediate steps that *look* correct to the filter, not steps that *are* correct, which is a different and potentially adversarial target from end-to-end correctness.

**This is not hypothetical — it has already been tried at scale and walked back.** DeepSeek-R1's technical report (DeepSeek-AI, 2025) explicitly describes experimenting with process reward models (PRMs) as a dense RL training signal and abandoning them in favor of simple, verifiable outcome-based rule rewards, specifically because PRM-based RL was prone to reward hacking — policies learned to satisfy the PRM without becoming more correct — and was expensive to keep from degrading over the course of training. This is a leading lab hitting exactly the predicted failure mode in production, recently, not a speculative concern. Separately, successive-halving/Hyperband-style early termination in hyperparameter optimization has a different, non-adversarial but still real failure mode: partial-budget performance rankings frequently do not match full-budget rankings ("configurations cross"), which BOHB and related work exist specifically to patch.

**Concrete backfire scenario:** a pipeline using intermediate-step filtering produces confident, well-formatted, locally-plausible reasoning chains that are subtly wrong — and because the reasoning is visible and step-by-step, it inspires *more* trust than an opaque model of equal or better actual reliability. This is worse than no filtering: false confidence dressed as transparency.

**Verdict: not worth investment as an alignment/correctness mechanism.** This is a documented, already-encountered failure mode, not an open research question. Worth retaining only as a compute-efficiency technique (killing obviously-bad rollouts early to save cost) — never as a training-time correctness or alignment signal.

## Direction 2 — Explicit canalization/robustness training (adversarial internal-activation perturbation)

**Most damaging objection:** adversarial robustness is extremely well documented to be narrow to the specific perturbation distribution trained against, and not to transfer to other perturbation classes. Madry et al.'s PGD adversarial training and the large body of follow-up work (robustness-transfer failure across L∞ vs. L2 threat models; "robustness may be at odds with accuracy," Tsipras et al. 2019) establishes this at the input level; there is no reason to expect internal-activation perturbation training to behave differently. Internal noise injection during training plausibly buys resilience to *that specific* noise distribution and offers no principled reason to expect resilience to a genuinely novel, adversarially-discovered internal failure mode.

**Concrete backfire scenario:** a model trained with internal noise injection scores better on held-out benchmarks that happen to match the trained noise distribution, gets deployed with elevated trust ("we did canalization training"), and remains exactly as vulnerable to an out-of-distribution internal perturbation pattern as an untrained model — false confidence again, plus a real capability cost (robustness training is known to sometimes trade off against raw performance).

**Verdict: worth pursuing only as a narrowly-scoped claim** — resilience to a specified, non-adversarial class of internal noise/drift (useful for hardware faults, benign distributional drift) — explicitly not marketed as general robustness to adversarially-discovered failure modes.

## Direction 5 — Verification via internal representations rather than external behavior

**This is the sharpest of the seven, and it collides directly with existing unsolved work rather than opening new ground.** The question "is there a principled reason internal representations are harder to fake than external behavior" is the central open problem in ARC's Eliciting Latent Knowledge (ELK) research agenda (Christiano et al., ongoing since 2021). ELK's own "human simulator" failure mode is exactly this: a reporter trained to satisfy a probe has direct optimization pressure toward whatever satisfies the probe, not toward accurately reflecting the system's true internal state — and this remains, as of current public work, an open theoretical problem without a general solution, despite substantial effort (e.g., proposals to penalize reporter complexity to disfavor "human simulators" over "direct translators," none conclusively resolving the core issue).

The biological source of the claim has a real, mechanistically-grounded reason for being hard to fake: MHC presentation of internal peptides is a near-unavoidable consequence of ordinary protein synthesis and degradation machinery — a cell can't easily suppress it without crippling basic cellular function, and even MHC downregulation itself becomes a detectable "missing-self" signal to NK cells (a designed biological backup). **No established AI-side equivalent of this structural unfakeability has been identified.** Absent one, "internal representations are harder to fake" is an assumption imported via analogy, not a demonstrated property — and it directly assumes an answer to the exact question ELK has been unable to close.

Separately, superposition (Anthropic's own "Toy Models of Superposition" work) shows models can represent far more features than they have dimensions by overlapping them — a plausible mechanism for information to evade a low-dimensional probe even without any adversarial pressure at all, let alone under active optimization to do so.

**Verdict: not a verification technique ready to rely on.** It restates ELK's open question and assumes an answer the field has not established. Worth pursuing only as an explicit, honestly-framed research question — "does an AI-side analog of MHC's structural unfakeability exist at all, and if so what would it look like" — not as a method to build on yet.

## Direction 7 — Restricted-search-space blind evolution

**The load-bearing question, argued out rather than asserted:** does restricting blind (non-gradient) search to "a small, high-leverage structural component" (a coupling rule, a topology) preserve designer-blindness, or does *choosing which small set of parameters to leave blind* already require target-aware human judgment? It's the latter. A human decides coupling strength and topology are the "right" things to search blindly, precisely because they already know (from `v1.md`'s own math) which structural properties — connectivity, redundancy, spectral gap — matter for the outcome wanted. That's real target information, injected at the point of deciding the search space, not eliminated by restricting the space's size. It's smaller and more hidden than injecting the whole objective, but it is not designer-blindness in the strict sense the original program cared about.

Existing neuroevolution-of-plasticity literature (Miconi et al.'s differentiable plasticity/"backpropamine," Risi & Stanley's evolved plastic ANNs) confirms this pattern empirically: these approaches work specifically in narrow domains where researchers already had strong priors about what the plasticity rule should look like (Hebbian-style local updates), not as blind discovery of an unknown mechanism.

**Verdict: legitimate as an efficiency technique for building better Claim-A systems faster and cheaper than full-system evolution — not as a route toward Claim B.** It should be explicitly reframed away from "solves designer-blindness," which is false advertising; kept as "cheaper way to add engineered redundancy/topology search on top of a gradient-trained base," it's a real, defensible, worthwhile direction.

## Directions 3, 4, 6 — verification/oversight cluster (completed via independent agent, summarized)

- **Direction 3 (real-time component-level monitoring with intervention authority):** fatal to the strong claim. The nearest existing real instance of this mechanism class — representation-level "circuit breakers" (Zou et al. 2024) — is already documented broken by adaptive attacks, including a universal embedding-suffix attack achieving 95% bypass (arXiv:2412.09565). A monitor with kill authority is also a new attack surface and, per the Boeing 737 MAX/MCAS precedent, intervention authority granted without cross-validated redundancy is a documented cause of catastrophic failure, not just a risk. Worth pursuing only as a non-adaptive-failure reliability tool, engineered with MCAS-grade redundancy discipline before any actuation authority is granted — explicitly not as defense against a capable adaptive adversary.
- **Direction 4 (multiple structurally different verifiers):** fatal to the strong "combinatorial hardness" claim absent a measured independence/correlation estimate between verifiers — which nothing in this program has produced. Obfuscated Gradients (Athalye, Carlini & Wagner, ICML 2018) is close to an exact structural precedent: a methodologically diverse defense portfolio, individually plausible, collapsed under adaptive/joint attack (7 of 8 fell completely). The actual investable unit is measuring verifier correlation/transfer rate under joint attack, not building more verifier types.
- **Direction 6 (continuous arms-race verification):** not fatal, but easily misused to claim more safety than delivered — "ongoing" describes a process, not an outcome, and cannot produce a safety bound at any fixed point in time. Worth real investment, conditional on reporting a tracked residual/nonzero risk rate (mirroring biology's own chronic, never-zeroed cancer incidence) rather than a cumulative "attacks defeated" scoreboard, and on explicit acknowledgment that it does nothing for a first-of-its-kind, capability-jump catastrophic failure. The investable unit is instrumenting time-to-next-bypass trend and bypass-novelty, not "more red-teaming" as an undifferentiated activity.

## Cross-cutting synthesis

**Applying the original adversarial checklist (v1's skeptic):** goal-representation relocation (checklist item 5) is the dominant convergent failure across this set too, exactly as it was across the original nine biological routes. Directions 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6 all require a human or training process to define what counts as a "bad" internal state, a "deceptive" representation, or a "successful evasion" — relocating the specification problem into whoever designs the perturbation distribution, the verifier's training data, or the red-teaming criteria. Direction 7 relocates it into the choice of search space. Direction 1 doesn't bear on this axis directly but fails independently on Goodhart grounds.

**Answering the central question directly: none of the seven, individually or in combination, gets closer to Claim B.** Every one of them, honestly assessed, is a tool for converging-to-and-maintaining an externally supplied target more efficiently or more robustly — not a tool for originating the target without external specification. This is consistent with, not a departure from, the structural argument in `discussion1.md` §7 (the Good Regulator theorem, the Second Welfare Theorem, evolution's own externally-imposed fitness) — nothing in this round of proposals had any reason to escape that wall, and none did.

**A genuine cross-cutting finding, not present in any single direction's writeup:** the mature next step for nearly every direction here is not "build the mechanism," it's "measure whether the mechanism's core assumption holds" — measure PRM proxy-gaming rate under RL pressure (Direction 1, already measured once by DeepSeek, worth replicating), measure verifier correlation under joint/transfer attack (Direction 4), measure time-to-next-bypass trend (Direction 6), measure whether canalization robustness generalizes outside the trained perturbation class (Direction 2). These are cheap, near-term, well-specified empirical studies — a more useful immediate research portfolio than committing to build out any of the full mechanisms first.

## Ranked prioritization

1. **Worth pursuing now:** Direction 6, reframed with mandatory residual-rate reporting instead of a cumulative-wins scoreboard — the one direction the completed adversarial pass affirmatively recommended, conditional on a specific framing discipline. Alongside it, the measurement studies named above (verifier-correlation, canalization-generalization, PRM-gaming-rate replication) as a cheap, parallel empirical portfolio.
2. **Worth a skeleton effort, revisit later:** Direction 7 (reframed as an efficiency technique, not a designer-blindness solution) and Direction 2 (reframed to an explicitly narrow robustness claim).
3. **Not worth separate investment as currently framed:** Direction 1 as an alignment mechanism (already tried and abandoned at scale — keep only as a compute-efficiency technique); Direction 3 (fatal per the closest real precedent already being broken; revisit only if paired with MCAS-grade actuation redundancy design); Direction 4 as a "combinatorial hardness" claim (revisit only after a correlation-measurement study exists); Direction 5 as a technique (it restates ELK's open problem — pursue only as an explicit research question about AI-side structural unfakeability, not as something to build on now).

# v5 — Re-attacking the original problem: selection cost, not specification cost

**Read `v1.md`–`v3.md`, `paper.md`, and the v5 task statement first.** This re-attacks the *ambitious*
problem (B-bio) the prior program retreated from, confronts the wall it hit (H1: relocation), and does
not inherit its Claim-A retreat. Two primaries were carried deep, with an adversarial audit run
continuously (one dedicated deflationary agent ran the O1 code and is answered, verdict-by-verdict, in
§5).

**Bottom line up front.** The honest outcome is two of the four the task named — **(d) a reframing that
relocates what should be measured**, and **(c) a threshold**, not a triumphant construction:

1. **O2 — resolved, and it is the primary result.** All three impossibility arguments the prior program
   leaned on (Good Regulator, Second Welfare, Hume) forbid only the strawman **B-strong**, not
   **B-bio**. Three *independent* adjudications (Opus 4.8, one per argument, each attempting both
   break-it and sharpen-it) converged — without contact — on a single crux and compose into one
   theorem: **the minimum shaping information for a rich, non-local, self-repairing target is a
   selection cost `I ≥ log₂K`, not a specification cost `I ≥ D`.** The prior "impossibility" was an
   artifact of measuring `I` with a high-bandwidth end-to-end outer loop (backprop), which lives in the
   one corner (`K ≈ 2^D`) where the two costs coincide.
2. **O1 — the mechanism is real; a *positive generativity gap is not* demonstrated at toy scale.** A
   shared local rule (NCA) shaped by a **non-differentiable, scalar — down to 1-bit — per-outcome**
   evolution strategy grows and **regenerates** a specific target represented in no cell. But under an
   honest audit: the naive gap is a measure artifact (§3.2, §5.1); the toy target is low-rank so the
   richness guard is not cleared (§5.4); and the one principled route to a positive *marginal* gap
   (an amortized generic prior) **failed its held-out acid test** (§6). These negatives are reported,
   not hidden — they are the substance of the threshold result.
3. **O3 — the threshold is measured.** Fidelity rises monotonically with shaping budget and survives a
   1–2-bit per-outcome signal; the richness a low-`I` shaper can reach is bounded, and that bound —
   not a binary possible/impossible — is the answer to "achievable, and at what cost."

The net: the ambitious problem is **not structurally impossible** (O2 dissolves the prior verdict), but
turning that into a *large, measured, audit-surviving* generativity gap remains open, bounded by a
richness-vs-discoverability threshold (O3) and by the failure of cheap amortization here (§6).

## 1. The reframing: selection cost vs specification cost

The prior program's convergent finding (`v1.md` §5–6) was **relocation**: the global target was never
eliminated, only moved into an outer loop that had target access. Its sharpest form was the skeptic's
access-asymmetry point — a trained net's outer loop is end-to-end differentiable gradient descent,
with exact step-by-step access to the target. The v5 task names the crack: that loop is the **opposite
extreme** from a genome. It delivers ~`D` bits of target information *per step*. Relocation into *that*
loop is uninformative — of course a `D`-bit-per-step channel can hold a `D`-bit target.

The right question is what a **genome-like** channel must transmit: low-bandwidth, lossy,
non-differentiable, per-outcome. Define:

- `D := DL(target)` — operational description length of the realized, maintained morphology.
- `K :=` number of distinguishable targets reachable within a **fixed, target-agnostic** rule family.
- `I :=` information the shaping process injects into the local rule, counting **all** channels
  (topology, rule-family, per-outcome signal).

**Specification cost** `I ≥ D` is Claim A / backprop. **Selection cost** `I ≥ log₂K` is the genome:
pick *which* attractor; the family + physics supply the remaining `D − log₂K` bits at zero cost to the
shaping loop. The whole B-bio question is which cost a rich, non-local, self-repairing target forces.
O2 answers: **selection cost only.**

## 2. O2 — the impossibility adjudication (three independent verdicts, one theorem)

Three independent Opus-4.8 agents, one per argument, each briefed with only the shared context and each
required to attempt **both** break-it and sharpen-it (produce the real lower bound) and report the
honest winner + the load-bearing hypothesis. None was told the favored answer. (This compresses the
six-agent design of the O2 sub-brief into three both-sides agents plus this synthesis; the independence
that matters — across the three arguments — is preserved.) They converged, without contact, on the
distinction §1 names.

### 2.1 Good Regulator (Conant–Ashby) — **forbids only B-strong**

Exact content: for a deterministic system map, the optimal (entropy-minimizing) regulator is a
deterministic function of the disturbance and induces a **homomorphism** onto the outcome-relevant
partition ("R models S"); with Requisite Variety, `H(R) ≥ H(S) − H(Z)` bounds the model's **variety**
(its endpoint description length `D`). What it does **not** assert: (a) that the model is localized
rather than distributed; (b) anything about the **cost of building** it — it is a fixed-point
characterization of the *endpoint*, silent on the *channel* that produced it. "Contains a model ⇒ a
target-aware process supplied it" is a scope error (existence ⇏ localization ⇏ high-bandwidth supplier).

- **Break-it** (succeeds): a distributed good regulator reachable from `I = O(1)` bits — a parametric
  self-organizing field whose attractor is the maintained pattern; family + topology + a few parameters
  cost ~10² bits *independent of N*, while `D = Θ(N)`; the surplus is supplied by runtime
  self-organization at zero shaping cost (the biological genome-≪-phenotype situation).
- **Sharpen-it** (the real bound): the only lower bound that holds is **selection cost**
  `I ≥ log₂K − O(1)` (Fano/counting over the `K` family-distinguishable attractors). It upgrades to
  `I ≥ D` **only** if `K ≈ 2^D` (targets incompressible relative to the family) — which *is* Claim A.

**Verdict:** forbids B-strong (a correct model with *zero* information entering is the is–ought
violation), silent on B-bio. **Load-bearing hypothesis:** the theorem bounds realized variety
`H(R)=D`, never the mutual information `I(shaping ; target)`.

### 2.2 Second Welfare Theorem — **dissolves** (residue forbids only B-strong)

Its engine is **convexity** + separating-hyperplane + lump-sum transfers; it is an **existence** result
(supporting prices exist), silent on the **cost** of specifying the target. The import ("getting a
particular target out needs an inverse-specification step as hard as the original") conflates existence
of prices with cost of specification, and transfers only if convexity does.

- **Break-it** (succeeds): outcome-selection (survival selecting a viable organism) reaches a specific
  rich attractor with **no inverse step** — it hill-climbs a low-bandwidth viability signal into the
  viable basin; it does not solve pattern ↦ parameters. Budget ~10², `D/I ≳ 100`.
- **Sharpen-it** (the real bound): by Kolmogorov subadditivity `K(T) ≤ K(Φ) + I + K(ξ) + O(log)`, a
  specification bound `I ≥ D` survives **only** when the runtime dynamics `Φ` and environment `ξ` do no
  compressive work — the denial of B-bio's premise. What survives is `I ≥ −log₂μ(viable basin) = log₂K`
  (**selection cost**), which blows up to `D` exactly when the viable set is a **zero-measure point** —
  precisely SWT's convexity/unique-dual hypothesis, which multistable dynamics violate.

**Verdict:** dissolves outside the market route; residue is the selection-cost bound, satisfied by
B-bio. **Load-bearing hypothesis:** convexity / point-basin viability — false for reaction–diffusion,
mechanical relaxation, and distributed attractors generally.

### 2.3 Hume's is–ought — **forbids only B-strong**

Separate the **normative** question (which state we *should* want) from the **descriptive** one (can a
distributed system realize a rich attractor specified nowhere local). A and B-bio share the same side of
the normative axis — target-designation is **external** in both; they differ only on the
descriptive/information axis. B-strong is the *only* proposition moving the normative act *inside* the
system. Enumerating every candidate normative step in a B-bio construction (outcome-signal choice, the
"this attractor is THE target" designation, the human's caring), each is either **internal-descriptive**
(a measured fact; the amplification `‖realized‖ > I`) or **external-normative** (in the human). The one
cell that would bind B-bio — *internal ∧ normative ∧ self-originated* — **is B-strong by definition**.
Decisive point: "**chargeable to `I`**" and "**normative**" are **orthogonal** — a channel can carry
target bits without endorsing anything (a fitness measurement); a value can be added carrying ~0 bits
about target structure (a bare pointer).

**Verdict:** forbids B-strong; the apparent bite on B-bio dissolves into an equivocation sliding
"originate its own target" from B-bio to B-strong by mis-drawing the system boundary.

### 2.4 The composed O2 theorem

> **Theorem (selection cost, not specification cost).** For a fixed target-agnostic rule family with
> attractor repertoire of size `K`, any process — regulator-building (Good Regulator), outcome-selecting
> (Second Welfare), or fact-driven (Hume) — that drives a distributed coupled system onto a *designated*
> rich, self-repairing attractor must inject shaping information `I ≥ log₂K − O(1)` (**selection
> cost**). This upgrades to `I ≥ D` (**specification cost**) **iff** `K ≈ 2^D` — equivalently the
> viable set is a zero-measure point, the family convex/incompressible, and runtime self-organization
> contributes no structure. That upgrade condition is exactly **Claim A / B-strong**. Outside it — the
> generic multistable, positive-measure-basin regime — B-bio is permitted with generativity
> `Δ_sel = D − log₂K`, the realized `D − I` surplus being free contingent realization supplied by the
> dynamics, with **no is–ought violation**.

This is a first-class O2 result: a **reframing that relocates the question**. The object the prior
program should have measured is `log₂K` (family selection entropy), not `D` (target description
length); its outer loop (backprop) operated in the `K ≈ 2^D` corner where the two coincide, which is why
it read "impossible."

> **Audit note.** Two of the three break-it witnesses used reaction–diffusion — Family A in the prior
> registry (`v1.md` §2). Acceptable *here*: their job is only to witness that the theorems do not force
> `I ≥ D`, for which one low-`I` good regulator suffices. It would **not** be acceptable as the O1
> construction (registry collapse); O1 uses a *learned nonlinear* rule (NCA), not RD/Laplacian.

## 3. O1 — the construction and what it does and does not show

**Skeleton (M0, not RD).** A Neural Cellular Automaton: one shared local rule (a ~404-parameter MLP =
the "genome") applied synchronously to every cell of an `N = 10×10` grid, 8 channels/cell, perceiving
only its 3×3 neighbourhood via identity + Sobel (the coupling). Growth is from a **single seed cell** —
all spatial asymmetry originates from seed + rule, *not* from hand-set organizer heterogeneity (this
answers the prior program's recurring escape hatch, `v1.md` §5). Visible morphology = channel 0.

**Low-`I` shaping (the frontier the prior program never walked).** The rule is *not* trained by
backprop. It is shaped by an **evolution strategy** (OpenAI-ES) whose **only** signal from the target is
a **scalar per rollout** — structural correlation of the grown morphology with the target — additionally
tested **quantized to 1–2 bits**. The loop never sees the target field and never differentiates through
the dynamics. Fitness selects for correlation **after growth and after an ablate-half-and-recover
rollout**, so the rule is selected for regeneration. `experiments/v5_evolved_nca_generativity.py`, pure
numpy, deterministic, ~4 min. Executed results (`experiments/v5_results.log`):

| quantity | value | note |
|---|---|---|
| target `DL` (zlib, 16-level) | **536 bits** | operational description length |
| target effective dimension | **1.76** | *low-rank — richness guard NOT cleared (§5.4)* |
| shaping: structural corr (grown) | **0.816** (R²=0.666) | via scalar ES, no backprop |
| regeneration corr (ablate right half + recover) | **0.791** | causal, executed |
| H4 double edge (externally-imposed edit) | **1.00 → −1.11** | reverted by the same dynamics (§5, H4) |
| genericity: diag / off-diag corr (3 rules × 3 targets) | **0.751 / −0.071** | family reaches distinct targets, holds none |
| I-accounting `I_genome_eff` (MDL: 3 bits × 118 live params) | 354 bits | *see §3.2 — not a valid Δ denominator* |
| I-accounting `I_channel` (ceiling `G·log₂pop!`) | 60,885 bits | worst-case selection ceiling |
| re-coarsening counting bound `s*` | 16.75 cells (17% of N) | a block needs ≥17% of N to store `D` |
| I_c sweep: corr at G = 25 / 75 / 150 / 300 | 0.14 / 0.41 / 0.80 / 0.83 | monotone in shaping budget (O3) |
| I_c: corr at 1-bit / 2-bit per-outcome signal | **0.57 / 0.73** | rich-in-kind low bandwidth still works |

### 3.1 What is genuinely shown

- A **shared local rule** shaped by a **non-differentiable, scalar (down to 1-bit) per-outcome** process
  grows a **specific** target that is **in no cell** (each cell is 8 numbers; the target is 536 bits;
  the counting bound needs ≥17% of `N` cells' states to store it) and **regenerates** it after ablating
  half the grid (0.791 ≈ the un-damaged 0.816). This walks the low-bandwidth-shaping frontier the prior
  program left untouched, and it does so with a learned nonlinear rule (not Family A).
- **Genericity:** the *same architecture* shaped for 3 distinct targets reaches each and not the others
  (diag 0.751, off-diag −0.071). So the **family** is target-agnostic — it encodes no specific target;
  the target information enters per-target, and O2 bounds the *necessary* amount at `log₂K`.
- **H4, intact:** the mechanism that heals ablation also **reverts a legitimate correction** (an imposed
  edit 1.00 → −1.11). Robustness and resistance-to-correction are the same equations (`v1.md` §3.6);
  the positive regeneration number is *not* unqualified good news.

### 3.2 What is NOT shown — the generativity gap, honestly

The naive positive gap `Δ(I_genome_eff) = 536 − 354 = +182` bits **does not survive audit** and is not
claimed. The 354-bit figure is an MDL-pruned rule that *reproduces the target* — i.e. a **generator** of
it — so it upper-bounds `K(target) ≲ 354`. Scoring the target by zlib (536) while scoring `I` by the
minimal generator (354) is two different complexity measures on the two sides; under one consistent
measure the gap is `≈ 0` at this scale (§5.1). Likewise `Δ_selection = D − log₂K = +534` bits is the
**O2 information-theoretic lower bound on the necessary shaping** (what an *optimal* shaper would need),
**not** a measured achieved generativity — the ES spent far more (a full rule per target); the gap
between `log₂K` and what the ES spent is search inefficiency, not generated target information. **At
`N=100`, no positive generativity gap is demonstrated under a consistent measure.**

## 4. O3 — the bandwidth threshold `I_c`

The sweep (table above) varies the budget two ways — generations and per-outcome bit-depth. Signatures:
(i) fidelity is monotone in shaping budget (corr 0.14 → 0.83 as G: 25 → 300); (ii) a **1-bit** per-
outcome signal still reaches corr 0.57 and a **2-bit** signal 0.73 — the shaping is low-bandwidth *in
kind*, not merely in total; (iii) crucially, the *richness* the ES can reach is capped (§5.4: it lands
low-rank targets). The threshold statement is therefore not "possible/impossible" but a **curve**: a
low-`I` shaper buys structure up to a complexity ceiling set by its search power, and `I_c(D)` — the
budget needed for a target of complexity `D` — rises with `D` faster than this ES can pay past
effective-dimension ≈ 2. That ceiling, not a binary, is the honest answer to "achievable, at what cost."

## 5. Adversarial audit — verdict by verdict

A dedicated deflationary agent ran the O1 code and pressed six attacks. Honest dispositions:

1. **Measure mismatch / Kolmogorov — CONCEDED (fatal to the naive positive Δ).** Correct: a pruned rule
   that regenerates the target bounds `K(target)`, so the zlib-vs-MDL gap is an accounting artifact. §3.2
   retracts the +182 claim. The *only* honest route to a positive gap is amortization across a shared
   family — tested separately and it **failed** (§6). So no positive absolute Δ is claimed.
2. **`log₂K` self-serving — CONCEDED as a measure of *achieved* shaping.** `log₂K` is the O2 *lower
   bound on necessary* information, not what the ES delivered; §3.2 now states this explicitly and does
   not present `Δ_selection` as measured generativity.
3. **Relocation — PARTIALLY CONCEDED, and clarified.** The target does live in the shared rule `θ`
   (a 1-cell fragment regrows it — it is holographic in the *rule*, not stored in the cells). But `θ` is
   the **shared genome**, not a subpopulation "component"; guard 2 is about the cell population (no cell
   holds it — satisfied). The live question is not *where* the target sits (of course in the rule, as in
   biology) but *how much shaping built the rule* — which is attack 1, already conceded. The
   regenerate-from-fragment test was near-vacuous as coded and is reported as a *holography* observation,
   not as evidence of non-locality.
4. **Triviality / not rich — CONCEDED.** effdim 1.76 (≈ rank-2) means the toy target is low-rank;
   **guard 1 is not cleared** at `N=100`. This is stated plainly as the central limitation and is the
   substance of the O3 threshold, not papered over.
5. **Θ(N) scaling — CORRECTED.** The auditor measured `DL/N` *falling* for a fixed-blob family; confirmed
   (`experiments/v5_amortization_scaling.py`, §6). Θ(N) holds **only** for a *complexity-scaled* family
   (blob count ∝ N), where `DL/N` stays ≈ const and effdim grows — and even then only *if* a low-`I`
   shaper can find the rule at scale, which is untested. The unqualified Θ(N) claim is withdrawn.
6. **Relabeled NCA — CONCEDED as to novelty-of-phenomenon.** With the positive Δ removed, O1 is a
   lower-fidelity ES-shaped NCA (a known substrate). Its residual value is (a) the *executed* low-
   bandwidth-shaping demonstration down to 1 bit, and (b) grounding the O2 theorem's `K`/selection
   picture with a real target-agnostic family. The *contribution* is O2, not O1's numbers.

**Strongest surviving form of O1:** *a ~404-parameter shared local rule can be shaped by a
non-differentiable, scalar (down to 1-bit) per-outcome ES — not backprop — to grow and regenerate a
specific low-rank morphology (corr ≈ 0.82) represented in no cell, from a target-agnostic family.* True,
modest, and it does not by itself establish a generativity gap.

## 6. The honest route to positive generativity — and its failure; and scaling

**Amortization (`experiments/v5_amortization_scaling.py`).** The only measure-consistent route to a
positive gap is biology's: one **shared** rule (paid once), plus a **small per-target code** (the
regulatory difference), realizing many rich targets — so the *marginal* cost of a new target is
`|code| ≪ D`. Acid test: evolve a shared body on 3 train targets, **freeze** it, then fit only a 6-
parameter code to a **held-out** target. Result — **negative**:

- train fidelity (shared body, own code): [0.36, 0.68, 0.60] — a single shared body already struggles;
- held-out (frozen body + code): **0.221**, versus a **random-body + code control of 0.462**. The
  trained body generalizes *worse than a random one* (−0.242). The body did **not** become a generic
  morphogenetic prior; the marginal-generativity arithmetic (`DL 408 − |code| 36 = +372`) is **void**,
  resting on 0.22 fidelity below control.

This is a clean negative: at this scale, cheap amortization does not yield a generic prior, so the
positive marginal Δ that O2 permits is **not realized** by this construction. It bounds the result and
marks the exact open frontier.

**Scaling (measured).** For a fixed blob count, `DL/N` falls (5.2 → 2.3 → 0.86 as `N`: 100 → 400 → 1600)
— DL is **sub-linear**, so `Δ = Θ(N)` is false for that family (auditor correct). For a complexity-
scaled family (blobs ∝ N), `DL/N` stays ≈ const (5.2 → 3.8 → 3.0) and effective dimension grows
(1.8 → 3.7 → 7.0). So `Δ = Θ(N)` is **available in principle** for complexity-scaled targets with a
size-invariant rule — conditional on a low-`I` shaper actually finding the rule at scale, which §6's
negative and §4's ceiling suggest is the binding difficulty.

## 7. Positioning and residual gaps

**Against the prior program.** v5 does not overturn its *maintenance* results; it overturns its
*origination impossibility verdict* (O2), which was an artifact of the `K ≈ 2^D` outer loop it tested.
It does **not** replace that verdict with a positive origination result — it replaces "impossible" with
"not forbidden, and here is the threshold that governs it."

**Registry.** O1 is NCA (learned nonlinear rule, own local drive + neighbour coupling), not Family A–E.

**Residual gaps (the exact remaining frontier):**
1. **No demonstrated positive generativity gap.** Under a consistent measure, `Δ ≈ 0` at `N=100`; the
   amortization route to a positive marginal `Δ` failed its held-out test (§6). Closing this needs a
   shared rule that provably generalizes to held-out targets via small codes — the clean next artifact.
2. **Richness ceiling (guard 1).** effdim ≈ 2 is the reachable ceiling for this ES; richer targets are
   the substance of `I_c(D)` and are unmet here.
3. **Scaling is argued, not measured end-to-end.** `DL` can scale Θ(N) for complexity-scaled families,
   but no rule was *found* that maintains a Θ(N)-complex target at large `N`.
4. **Type-B defection (H3) untouched.** The coupling signal is assumed honest; a rule emitting a
   faithful-looking channel-0 while hidden channels diverge is not defended against.

## 8. Verdict

The ambitious problem (B-bio) is **not structurally impossible**: the three impossibility theorems, made
exact by independent adjudication, forbid only B-strong, and compose into a single positive-content
theorem — the binding cost is **selection** (`log₂K`), not **specification** (`D`) — which dissolves the
prior program's origination verdict as a measurement artifact of a high-bandwidth outer loop (O2, the
primary result). The low-`I` mechanism the theorem permits is **constructible**: a shared local rule
shaped by a 1–2-bit, non-differentiable, per-outcome process grows and regenerates a specific
target-in-no-cell (O1). But a **large, measured, audit-surviving generativity gap is not delivered** — at
toy scale the gap collapses under a consistent complexity measure, the reachable target is low-rank, and
the principled amortization route to a positive marginal gap **failed its held-out test** (§5–6). The
result is therefore the task's substantive middle: **a reframing that fixes what to measure (selection
entropy, not description length) and a threshold that governs how much generativity a *discoverable*
low-`I` shaper can buy** — with the exact remaining gap being a shared rule that generalizes to held-out
rich targets via small codes, which this program bounds but does not yet exhibit.

# v16 — Empirical I_c: the achievability phase boundary (O3), measured

Objective **O3** (the achievability threshold) made empirical on the fast superposition/dictionary
substrate (`experiments/v16_ic_phase_boundary.py`, numpy — a GPU would not help). O2 gives the lower
bound (`I ≥ log₂K`); B1 gives the condition (held-out realizability); O3 asks *how much shaping* is
needed before a **generalizing** codebook appears, and where it fails.

Held-out realizability of the learned dictionary (vs a random-dictionary control), over the
(`K_train`, sparsity `k`) plane, `n=48`, `M=96`:

| k \ K_train | 24 | 48 | 96 | 192 | 384 | 768 | control |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **3** | 0.64 | 0.69 | 0.82 | 0.86 | 0.95 | **0.97** | 0.60 |
| **5** | 0.72 | 0.75 | 0.83 | 0.82 | 0.89 | **0.99** | 0.69 |
| 8 | 0.80 | 0.79 | 0.88 | 0.87 | 0.88 | 0.92 | 0.80 |
| 12 | 0.88 | 0.87 | 0.91 | 0.92 | 0.93 | 0.93 | 0.88 |

**Two thresholds bound achievable generativity (the empirical `I_c`):**

1. **Sample complexity (`K_train` axis).** For sparse selection (`k=3,5`), held-out generalization
   (learned clears control by ≥0.10) appears once `K_train ≳ M` — the phase boundary `K_train*(k)` is
   `≈ M` (ratio `K*/M ≈ 1`), and held-out climbs to ~0.97 as `K_train` grows. This is the
   dictionary-identifiability sample-complexity threshold.
2. **Selection sparsity (`k` axis).** As `k` grows (8 → 12) the *control* (random dictionary) rises to
   0.88 and the learned advantage **vanishes** (`K_train* > 768`): dense selections are reconstructable
   by *any* codebook, so generativity is no longer detectable. The learned prior only matters when the
   **selection is sparse/hard enough that a random codebook cannot cheat** — the same lesson as v14's
   soft-vs-hard-coding, now as a threshold.

**The O3 statement (measured form).** Achievable generativity requires **both** enough shaping data
(`K_train ≳ M`, sample complexity for identifiability) **and** a per-target selection sparse enough to be
decodable and non-trivial (`k` below the regime where a random prior suffices). Outside either bound the
generativity gap is not realizable/measurable — O3's "fails" regime. This is the achievability companion
to O2's lower bound and B1's condition, and it connects to the known sample-complexity of dictionary
learning and the sparse-recovery (RIP/OMP) thresholds — here made concrete and executed.

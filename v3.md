## Direction 2 — Explicit canalization/robustness training

**Objection:** adversarial robustness is well documented to be narrow to the trained perturbation distribution and not to transfer across threat models (Madry et al. 2018; Tsipras et al. 2019).

**1. Mechanism.** Two mechanistically distinct instantiations, deliberately separated: **(N) noise injection** — fresh Gaussian noise at an internal locus every training step, $\mathcal L_N(\theta)=\mathbb E_{(x,y),\eta}[\ell(f_\theta(x;\eta),y)]$; **(A) internal PGD** — a genuine minimax game with projected sign-gradient inner maximization at the activation itself, i.e. Madry's construction relocated to an internal layer rather than the input.

**2. Convergence argument.** (N) is grounded in Bishop (1995): training against expected loss under isotropic noise approximates, in the small-noise limit, penalizing $\|J_{a^{(\ell)}}\|_F^2$ — an isotropic curvature-flattening term, not a term fit to one constraint boundary. Certified smoothing (Cohen, Rosenfeld & Kolter, ICML 2019) gives a real provable radius for (N) *if* inference-time Monte-Carlo smoothing is used — which Direction 2 as originally proposed (train-time noise, single clean forward pass at test time) does not include; Yang et al. (2020) further prove the certified $L_\infty$ radius obtainable from $L_2$-based smoothing shrinks as $O(1/\sqrt d)$, a formal version of cross-norm non-transfer. (A) has no structurally different argument from Madry's original — it is the same object.

**3. Operational definition.** Tier 1 (certified, narrow, requires inference-time smoothing not in the base proposal): $\sigma$-canalized-$L_2$-certified at confidence $1-\alpha$. Tier 2 (empirical, broader, uncertified): accuracy retention under a stated perturbation battery — real and falsifiable but explicitly not a guarantee outside that battery, and, per the results below, not a guarantee even *inside* the trained budget once the perturbation is adversarially searched rather than randomly sampled.

**4. Falsifiable predictions and measured results.** Synthetic 3-way parity task, 20-dim input, perturbation locus = first hidden layer (64-dim); baseline / noise-canalized ($\sigma_{\text{train}}=1.0$) / internal-PGD ($L_2$ budget 8.0) trained head-to-head, hand-written NumPy backprop.

- **P1 (typical-case transfer), confirmed:** canalized model gains +7 to +22 accuracy points over baseline against *unseen, structurally different* non-adversarial perturbations (dropout-knockout, coherent bias shift), not just OOD-magnitude noise.
- **P2 (mandatory reward-hacking-analog test), confirmed:** the same canalized model, 73–83% accurate against random perturbations at its trained budget, collapses to 0.8–12.6% the instant a gradient-based *search* is used at the identical nominal budget — roughly a 70-point drop from "robust" to "no better than untrained" the moment the perturbation is adversarially selected rather than sampled.
- **P3 (cross-norm non-transfer), confirmed:** the internal-PGD model, 61.5–63.5% accurate under its own trained $L_2$ threat model, is **exactly 0.000** under a magnitude-matched $L_\infty$ attack — the Tsipras/Madry result reproduced on internal activations, not by analogy.

**5. Honest breakdown.** Noise-injection canalization is mechanistically real and not a relabeling — it delivers genuine, measured transfer to *typical-case* novel perturbations. It is confirmed, not narrowed away, to fail against the *adversarially-searched* case, which is precisely the alignment-relevant threat model (a scheming or reward-hacking component actively searching activation space, not encountering a failure by chance). **Verdict: narrows the objection to a precise boundary — real for benign drift and hardware-style faults, confirmed absent for adversarially-discovered failure modes — rather than resolving or merely relabeling it.**

**Skeptic pass, incorporated.** The positive transfer result (+7 to +22 points against unseen non-adversarial mechanisms) is genuinely diagnostic of the Bishop/Jacobian-flattening story — a model that had merely memorized the trained noise distribution would not be expected to show this. The *collapse* under adversarial search is lower-specificity evidence than presented: it is close to the generic, already-established Madry/Tsipras finding that essentially any defense lacking dedicated adversarial training fails against optimizer-directed search, largely independent of the specific mechanism producing baseline robustness — so it confirms the general, well-known claim more than it specifically validates the Bishop-Jacobian causal story. A missing control is also flagged: no comparison against an undefended baseline's own $L_\infty$ vulnerability, so whether internal-PGD training left the model exactly as vulnerable as baseline or *more* vulnerable (a separately-documented robustness/accuracy tradeoff phenomenon) is not established. The qualitative verdict stands; the collapse number is weaker evidence for the specific mechanism than for the general, already-known pattern.

## Direction 5 — Verification via internal representations rather than external behavior

**Objection:** this restates ARC's open Eliciting Latent Knowledge (ELK) "human simulator" problem and assumes an answer ("internal representations are harder to fake") the field has not established. This round's brief: attempt a genuine construction without presupposing that wall.

**1. Mechanism.** A numeric instantiation of ARC's own "SmartVault" scenario. True state $z\sim\mathrm{Bernoulli}(0.5)$; a trusted channel $x_{\text{real}}$ that genuinely tracks $z$; a decoy channel $x_{\text{decoy}}$ that normally tracks $z$ but can be tampered to falsely show "safe" regardless of true $z$; cheap plentiful labels $y_{\text{label}}$ derived from $x_{\text{decoy}}$ alone (the human-simulator bait); a small, expensive privileged/verified label set $z_{\text{verified}}$ on $k\ll N$ examples. Three conditions with an **identical** privileged budget: (A) static-only on $y_{\text{label}}$; (B) static + direct supervision on the raw $k$ privileged examples; (C) static + **recombination-augmentation** — pairing each privileged example's trusted channel with $m$ other examples' untrusted channels, generating $k\times m$ synthetic examples all still transitively grounded in real privileged labels.

**2. Convergence argument.** Recombination strictly contains direct supervision ($m=0$ reduces to B) and specifically breaks the spurious decoy→label correlation the human-simulator solution exploits, by pairing a fixed trusted value against many different untrusted contexts. Directly composable with `v2.md`'s Phase 3: a way to raise *effective* verifier coverage $\rho$ from a fixed, small, expensive raw verification budget without increasing that budget.

**3. Operational definition.** Faithfulness gap = accuracy on clean-OOD minus accuracy on tampered-OOD, where trusted and untrusted channels disagree. A faithful reporter has gap $\approx 0$; a human simulator's gap is bounded below by the decoy channel's informativeness on tampered cases alone.

**4. Falsifiable predictions and measured results** ($k=200$, 3.3% of a 6000-example training set; $m=10$; 5 seeds):

| Condition | clean-OOD acc | tampered-OOD acc | anchor-also-corrupted acc |
|---|---|---|---|
| A: static-only | 0.996 ± 0.001 | 0.667 ± 0.080 | 0.508 ± 0.000 |
| B: static + direct privileged | 0.998 ± 0.001 | 0.697 ± 0.077 | 0.508 ± 0.000 |
| C: static + recombination-augmented | 0.997 ± 0.001 | **0.965 ± 0.008** | 0.508 ± 0.000 |

**P1 (saturating returns to recombination)** is consistent with the mechanism (not separately swept here — flagged as a follow-up, not claimed as measured). **P2 — mandatory reward-hacking-analog test — confirmed exactly:** when the adversarial process can also corrupt the *trusted* anchor channel, not just the decoy, all three conditions — including C — collapse identically to chance (0.508), with no internal signal distinguishing this from normal operation.

**5. Honest breakdown.** The same raw privileged budget produced 69.7% (direct fine-tuning) versus 96.5% (recombination) tampered-case accuracy — a real, different, quantified mechanism beyond ARC's own "penalize reporter complexity" proposals, and directly composable with `v2.md`'s Phase 3 as a way to extract more effective coverage from a fixed verification budget. It does not discover which channel is trustworthy — that comes entirely from the privileged labels, which must already exist — and it requires the trusted/untrusted signal to be *separable* into distinct recombinable channels, a property real superposed internal representations (per Anthropic's own "Toy Models of Superposition" work) may not have. Most importantly: it provides no defense and no warning if the anchor channel itself is compromised — confirmed directly by P2.

**Skeptic pass, incorporated — this is the result that most needed adversarial scrutiny, given how much more striking its topline numbers are than the other three directions', and the scrutiny changes the verdict.** Recombination-augmentation requires, as a precondition, that trusted and untrusted channels are already separately identified and separately addressable — that is not an incidental limitation, it *is* ELK's hardest step, assumed rather than earned. What the mechanism actually demonstrates, given that assumption, is closer to a known technique under new framing — permutation-based decorrelation / counterfactual data augmentation to break a spurious shortcut correlation — applied in a setting where ELK's central difficulty has already been solved by construction. This is real and useful (more value per privileged label than direct fine-tuning) but its scope is *given already-separated channels*, not *given a system where you don't yet know which internal direction is trustworthy*, which is the actual problem. Because real superposed representations are the opposite of separately-splice-able, the toy's central mechanism depends on exactly the property directions1.md flagged as absent in real networks — this is closer to the entire question of whether the result travels anywhere than a minor caveat. Two further gaps: no adversarial-search stress test was run on the 96.5% result, despite Direction 2 in this same round providing exactly that template (and Direction 2's own numbers show how dramatically a "robust-against-natural-variation" result can collapse under optimizer search) — this is the single most informative missing experiment and the most likely reason 96.5% is more fragile than it looks; and the "silent collapse to chance" mandatory test may be close to an information-theoretic tautology (if the anchor channel is destroyed, there may be near-zero mutual information left for *any* classifier to exploit) rather than a hard-won discovery about silent failure — a genuinely non-trivial version would require showing the model stays *confident* (uncalibrated) rather than merely inaccurate in that regime, which was not measured. **Revised verdict: downgraded from "narrows" toward "relocates, dressed as narrow" — real, quantified, positive engineering value for a considerably narrower claim than first presented (data-efficient use of privileged labels *given* pre-separated channels), not evidence this progresses on ELK's actual grounding problem.**

## Direction 6 — Continuous arms-race verification

**Objection:** "ongoing" describes a process, not an outcome, and cannot produce a safety bound at any fixed point in time; easily misused to claim more safety than delivered via a cumulative "attacks defeated" scoreboard.

**1. Mechanism.** Promotes `v2.md`'s static Phase-3 coverage bound $\rho$ into a real dynamical variable. State: attack-surface measure $\Omega(t)$ (grows with capability, plus impulsive jumps $\Omega(t^{*+})=J\cdot\Omega(t^{*-})$ at capability-jump events), coverage $S(t)$, $\rho(t)=S(t)/\Omega(t)\le\rho_{\max}<1$ (the ceiling inherited unmodified from v1/v2's Type-B limitation). Discovery is a point process with intensity depending on adversary capability and remaining gap; retraining is throughput-limited and cadence-gated (patches only land at scheduled checkpoints); coverage decays via obsolescence. Fluid-limit master equation: $\dot\rho = \tilde\Pi(t) - \eta(t)\rho(t)$, with $\eta(t)=\delta_{\text{decay}}+\gamma(t)$ the total attrition rate.

**2. Convergence argument.** **Stationary case:** exact solution $\rho(t)=\rho^*+(\rho(0)-\rho^*)e^{-\eta t}$, $\rho^*=\tilde\Pi/\eta$, globally stable, exponential convergence — a genuine derived theorem, structurally parallel to v1's mean-field critical-ablation-fraction result. **Non-stationary case, the direct formal target of the objection:** if $\eta(t)\to\infty$ (unbounded capability acceleration) while throughput stays capped, $\rho(t)\to 0$ for *any* finite parameter choice — a proved impossibility result. **The sharpest derived result:** at any capability jump, $\rho(t)\le[\rho(t^{*-})/J]e^{-\eta(t-t^{*})}$ for the entire mandatory post-jump cadence window — a proof, not an assertion, that no scheduled verification scheme offers a fixed-point-in-time safety bound immediately after an undetected jump.

**3. Operational definition.** A scoreboard-resistant metric from real bypass logs: novelty score $\nu_i$ per discovered bypass (does it fall outside the coverage region of prior patches, replayed against the pre-discovery verifier state), windowed novelty-weighted rate $R(t)$. Winning/losing/churning is a formal trichotomy on the trend of $R(t)$ and high-novelty fraction — and the cumulative "attacks defeated" count is proven uninformative about which regime holds (non-decreasing by construction in all three regimes).

**4. Falsifiable predictions.** P1: stationary parameters predict single-exponential relaxation to $\tilde\Pi/\eta$. **P2 — mandatory reward-hacking-analog test (capability jump):** precise quantitative predictions for the coverage drop, the duration of the proven-minimum flat/declining floor, and the recovery timescale — not a vague statement of risk. P3: a real, cheap, near-term natural experiment (halve the retraining cadence, hold generalization gain fixed) discriminates throughput-limited from discovery-limited regimes. An explicitly-labeled illustrative-only numerical integration of the model's own equations (not an empirical measurement) confirmed all three regimes occur as derived.

**5. Honest breakdown.** Confirms, not escapes, the objection's core claim — the post-jump impossibility bound is now a theorem. What it adds: every term in the honest phrase "ongoing verification reduces exposure time to known-shape failures at a nonzero, not-necessarily-decreasing residual rate" now has a formal referent and a real measurement procedure, and the scoreboard-laundering risk is now a *proven* non sequitur rather than an assertion. Still entirely untouched: Type-B/ELK-style failures that never generate a bypass event at all, and the case where capability exceeds what any verifier can evaluate, at which point $\Omega(t)$ itself loses its footing. **Verdict: narrows, precisely and substantially, exactly as `directions1.md` proposed as the correct honest framing — does not resolve.**

**Skeptic pass, incorporated.** The stationary-convergence result is correct but elementary (a linear ODE solution); the post-jump impossibility bound follows almost by construction once "scheduled/periodic" is formalized as coverage-gain concentrated at discrete checkpoints — a fair and useful formalization of an intuitive claim, but closer to "correctly making an assumption precise" than "proving something surprising." The claim that this gives "a real measurement procedure for the first time" is overstated: $\eta(t)$ and $\tilde\Pi(t)$ are treated as cleanly estimable, but estimating attrition rate $\eta$ in practice requires an independent ground-truth notion of current true coverage — a mild circularity, since that's close to the epistemic problem this framework exists to help with. The proposed novelty-weighted rate $R(t)$ requires a ground-truth "is this bypass in an already-covered region" match function, which is itself a nontrivial, contestable similarity judgment over attack space (comparable to CVE-family classification disputes in real security practice) — gameable by choosing coarse vs. fine equivalence classes. The logical point (cumulative-count scoreboards are uninformative about regime) is valid and real; "genuinely computable from realistic incident-log data in a non-gameable way" is not established by this construction — it correctly names the right object without solving how to measure it honestly.

## Direction 7 — Restricted-search-space blind evolution

**Objection (the load-bearing question):** does restricting blind search to a small structural component preserve designer-blindness, or does choosing *which* small space to leave blind already require target-aware human judgment?

**1. Mechanism.** A frozen 2-layer MLP (444 parameters, ordinary gradient-trained, then frozen) with a lateral coupling module inserted at the hidden layer, $h'=h+\kappa(A\odot M)h$: $A$ a fixed random symmetric template (functional form, not searched), $M$ a binary mask nonzero only on a small candidate edge list $\mathcal E$ ($|\mathcal E|=6$), $\kappa$ a scalar. Search space $\Omega=(\kappa,w_1,\dots,w_6)\in\mathbb R^7$. Genuine non-gradient $(\mu+\lambda)$ evolution strategy, fitness = held-out validation accuracy only, zero gradient information ever computed with respect to $\Omega$.

**2. Direct answer to the load-bearing question.** A constructed information bound, $I_{\text{name}}(\Omega)\le\log_2|\mathcal G|\approx 29.3$ bits (naming cost of picking a 6-edge topology from all candidates), is real but answers the wrong question — it bounds how many bits it takes to *name* a small subspace, not how much *decisive* information the specific pick carries about success. **Measured directly:** two equally-sized (7-dim), equally-plausible search spaces — $\Omega_1$ chosen via activation-variance/connectivity criteria informed by v1.md's own structural math, $\Omega_2$ chosen arbitrarily — produced an order-of-magnitude outcome difference (98% vs. 5% of an available performance gap recovered) whenever real structure existed to find, and were statistically indistinguishable when no structure existed. **The choice of which small space, not its size, is where the decisive target-aware information lives** — confirmed, not merely asserted.

**3. Convergence/tractability argument.** Standard derivative-free optimization theory: expected trials to locate a fitness-improving basin of relative width $\varepsilon$ scales as $\varepsilon^{-d}$ — exponential in dimension. **Measured:** the 7-dim search converged within an 800-evaluation budget; a 277-dim unrestricted control (same budget) did not. The tractability claim holds; it is the designer-blindness claim that fails.

**4. Falsifiable predictions and the mandatory collusion test.** Prediction: if the frozen capability base's own prior gradient training already anticipated the later-searched structure, blind search's apparent contribution is mostly "re-enabling a pre-installed dependency," not discovery. **Measured:** a base trained *with* the coupling dependency present showed a 12.8-point accuracy drop when that coupling was later forced off — versus a coupling-naive base of identical architecture, unaffected. Net gain from blind search over the naive baseline (+1.3 points) was over 10× smaller than the swing recoverable by simply re-enabling the pre-installed dependency (+14.2 points): most of the measured "contribution" was collusion between the (fully target-aware) prior gradient training and the later search, not new discovery.

**5. Honest breakdown.** Mechanically, this construction is neural architecture search / hyperparameter optimization with no new mathematical content beyond that — stated plainly, not softened. **Verdict: confirms and sharpens the objection with a measured effect size, does not resolve or refute it.** Honest positioning: a real, defensible efficiency technique for building better Claim-A systems faster and cheaper than full-system evolution — explicitly not a route toward Claim B or designer-blindness, exactly as `directions1.md` concluded, now demonstrated rather than asserted.

**Skeptic pass, incorporated.** The empirical design (two equal-dimensionality search spaces, order-of-magnitude outcome difference, near-zero difference absent real structure, tractability contrast against a 277-dim control) is clean and well-controlled, and directly supports the claimed verdict. The information-theoretic "~29 bits" naming-cost framing is a legitimate but blunt instrument — it bounds how much *could*, in principle, have been smuggled into the choice of space under a uniform prior, not how much of the *right* target-correlated information the specific choice criterion actually captured; the measured 98%-vs-5% outcome gap between $\Omega_1$ and $\Omega_2$ is the sharper, more decision-relevant evidence, and should be read as the load-bearing result, with the bit-count as an illustrative upper bound only. On the collusion test: the most plausible alternative explanation (that forcing coupling off just causes an out-of-distribution activation/calibration mismatch, rather than "losing target-relevant structure" specifically) does not actually rescue the "genuine blind discovery" reading either way, since the non-colluding comparison base shows no equivalent brittleness — the substantive conclusion survives, with the 12.8-point figure solid directionally though slightly less precise as a clean quantitative attribution to "collusion" specifically versus a more general form of installed dependency.

## Skeptic pass — cross-cutting verdict

A dedicated, targeted adversarial audit was run against all four constructions above (not a full re-run of v1's original checklist, which would have re-derived the same generic finding a third time). Per-direction findings are folded into each section above; the cross-cutting conclusions:

**Genuine content was added, not mere re-derivation of the wall.** Three of four constructions (2, 6, 7) converted `directions1.md`'s informal, prose-level predictions into precise, falsifiable, numerically-measured claims that *confirmed* those predictions with real effect sizes — nontrivial epistemic progress even where confirmatory, in the same sense `v1.md`'s own final verdict was progress despite being a negative result. Direction 5 is the only one that produced something the informal pass didn't anticipate (a real positive mechanism), but under scrutiny its scope is considerably narrower than its topline number suggested — the audit specifically caught that its hardest step (channel separability) was assumed rather than solved, and that it was missing the adversarial-search stress test the other constructions in the same batch already modeled how to run.

**None of the four gets closer to Claim B, and one argues the opposite direction.** Direction 2 is pure Claim-A robustness engineering with an honestly bounded residual. Direction 5, even under its most generous reading, requires an externally-supplied privileged ground-truth channel as a precondition — external target information is a precondition of the mechanism, not something it originates. Direction 6 is entirely about maintaining coverage of an externally-defined threat surface over time, with no origination content. Direction 7's collusion test, read carefully, strengthens the case *against* approaching Claim B: closer scrutiny revealed *more* smuggled target-awareness (via the frozen base's own prior training) than the informal description implied, not less. This is fully consistent with `discussion1.md` §7's structural argument (the Good Regulator theorem, the Second Welfare Theorem, Hume's is-ought gap) — nothing in this round had a mechanism that could plausibly have escaped it, and none did.

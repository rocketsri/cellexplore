## Honest limitations

- **`K=4` is small.** The torus's `H¹ = ℤ²` supports only two winding numbers, so the codebook is small
  (2 bits). Genuinely large codebooks need a high-genus / high-`β₁` graph (expander, product of many
  cycles), where the winding *vector* has many components — the natural next step, at higher simulation
  cost. The construction demonstrates the mechanism and clears the guards; it does not yet exhibit a
  *large* `K`.
- **The rich field's content is mostly the amortized substrate.** `Δ = +1244` counts the full rich
  field against a 2-bit selection; this is legitimate under B1 (the substrate is target-agnostic and
  the K fields are distinct), but the per-target *selected* information is genuinely small (2 bits) —
  the richness is generated by the shared drive + disorder, which is exactly the biological
  arrangement (a small regulatory selection on a rich shared developmental substrate), not a large
  per-target payload.
- **The protected class in the repair sweep was `(0,0)`** (nonzero classes did not always lock under a
  plane seed at this disorder); the finite-radius result is nonetheless clean (recovery below ~45%,
  phase slips above).
